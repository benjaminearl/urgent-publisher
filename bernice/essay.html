Non-Violent Acts of Resistance, Rise Up Singing

Protests, resistance to the oppression, have been documented going back as far as 650 BC. If today's global tensions are any indication of how much longer protests will continue, it looks like forever. Especially when the oppressors seems to have beans in their ears and blinders over their eyes.

Is showing up enough? How can individuals do more to support the cause, and how can the cause be more effective?

Songs play an important role in protests. There are songs that rally courage, songs that challenge, songs that enlighten, songs that make you cry, songs that make you smile, and songs that leave you feeling angry. Songs create a vibe. Both the true vibrations that are scientifically proven to calm the body and soul, and "vibe", the ever-shifting mood of a space that is created by the beings that inhabit it.
The "vibe" I'm supporting in this essay is calm. You know the one, it's the everybody-calm-down type of vibe. When tensions get high and emotions are explosive, a song can be a balm. Melody and rhythmn working together to blanket everyone in love and hope. But who will sing these songs? Who will lead the crowd and keep it going? Perhaps you would like to step up, sing along, and lead the crowd.
