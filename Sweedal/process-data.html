How do you check the weather in your daily life?





Before earth sciences and western weather forecasting came along, communities have survived through natural disasters and weather change because they did this one action.



They read and learnt the signs of nature.

Indigenous knowledge systems represent a systematic collection of understanding developed by local communities through accumulated experience, informal experimentation, and a deep connection with their environment, which enhances modern weather forecasting

Sheila Watt-Cloutier (Canadian Inuit activist) says, “What we're trying to teach is that traditional knowledge is not just for Indigenous people, it's for everybody… All you have to do, really, is start to respect and understand traditional knowledge of Indigenous peoples and you will see there will be a groundswell of new creative and innovative ways and means in which to address these challenges that we face today in the world.”

East Africa India
Coucal in Tanzania singing out loud is an indication of short rains Cuckoo in Rajasthan flying from north to south direction in early hour of the day 5am to 6am emitting loud vocalization - region receives rain the very 3rd day.
Goats, sheep, and cattle flapping their ears repeatedly at any time during the dry season indicates near onset of rains, and prospects of a good season. (Tanzania). Cattle body condition and behavior include licking of each other’s body, display of a relaxed mood and getting away from water points after drinking, normal reproductive behavior (including increased activity in bulls). (Uganda) Cows and Buffaloes: Farmers observed that when cattle rested less, swished their tails vigorously, or licked their forelegs repeatedly, it was a sign of rainfall. (Rajasthan) Goats andSheep: Their unusual restlessness and changes in grazing patterns often preceded rainfall. (Rajasthan and Himachal Pradesh).
In Uganda, croaking of frogs during the day (as cited 68% of households in Rakai and 43% in Hoima). Frogs emerge and croak to cope with the humid air caused by rain. Frogs and Toads: Intense croaking before monsoon showers is commonly regarded as an indicator of rainfall. 
Large numbers of army ants occurring almost everywhere, including within houses and appear to be “celebrating” is an indication of onset of mostly heavy rains during short-rain season. Ant lifting and shifting their egss - it may rain any time. Basically, Enlarged ant mounds or ants moving nests to higher ground suggested incoming rains.(Rajasthan).
Migration of bees during season of resource abundance from north to south is also a sign of drought, while migration in the reverse direction indicates a normal rainfall season. HoneyBees activity - Livestock restlessness.
Occurrence and migration specifically of the black butterflies from south to north is an indication of early onset of rains and a prospect of a good season. Presence of insects on Albizia trees with water dripping from them is an indication of a good season, almost a month before start of short rains. Dragonflies appearing in large numbers were considered signs of an imminent monsoon. Termites emergence from underground and Grasshopper presence on its aak - normal year.

The Climate Decoder

THE CLIMATE DECODER

As we face unprecedented challenges from climate change, the ability to read and interpret natural signs becomes increasingly important. By learning from animal behavior and respecting the accumulated wisdom of communities around the world, we can encourage people to adapt and take the environmental around them seriously.



In the face of the climate crisis, we are all trying to decode the signs of change. This project seeks to archive the animal behaviours of extreme weather patterns of the African Asian Subcontinents, and understand that these observations have proved to be talismans for their people for thousands of years. The climate change in the Northern Hemisphere of the globe shifts this prompt to question what signs might testify to be useful and encourage us to become effective readers of the natural signs of change.
