THE CLIMATE DECODER

As we face unprecedented challenges from climate change, the ability to read and interpret natural signs becomes increasingly important. By learning from animal behavior and respecting the accumulated wisdom of communities around the world, we can encourage people to adapt and take the environmental around them seriously.



In the face of the climate crisis, we are all trying to decode the signs of change. This project seeks to archive the animal behaviours of extreme weather patterns of the African Asian Subcontinents, and understand that these observations have proved to be talismans for their people for thousands of years. The climate change in the Northern Hemisphere of the globe shifts this prompt to question what signs might testify to be useful and encourage us to become effective readers of the natural signs of change.
